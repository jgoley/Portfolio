Resume Bio

Jonathan Goley aka 'J.Go'

I grew up learning valuable life skills from two older brothers such as DOS commands and how to kick a soccer ball. I enjoyed the luxury of attending the elementary school of which my father was principal. I gardened, cried when my cats died and wanted to be a teenage mutant ninja turtle.

High school exposed me to the two interests with which I have devoted much of my life: photography and programming. I developed a simple point of sale program in BASIC for an imaginary pizza joint and spent hours in the school's darkroom watching silver coalesce into an image.

Initially I chose programming as the interest I wanted to turn into a career. But, I had not matured out of the timid child I was. After a year as a computer science major I switched to studio art (photography). This degree lead to a job at the Columbia Museum of Art where I began as a preparator (art handler). Somewhere between installing Renoirs, Monets and other incomprehensibly priced artwork, I started working on the museum's website. This started me back on the track I left as a college freshman.

To satisfy my curiosity and respect for nature I became a beekeeper which began when a 3 pound package of honeybees (10-12 thousand stinging insects) was placed in the backseat of my car. Now I manage 13 hives and have branded myself as "JGo Bees." To satisfy my inner-Muir I've done epic solo backpacking trips in Glacier NP, Rocky Mountain NP and all over western NC. To satisfy my love of photography I taught myself a 19th-century process called wet plate collodion. The images are actually exposed on plates of glass. And to satisfy my other miscellaneous interests I listen to NPR religiously, play disc golf and play guitar (along with a few other instruments).

  • Parse (back-end)
